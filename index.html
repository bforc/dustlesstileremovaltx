What is Epoxy Flooring?

Epoxy flooring is a highly durable, resin-based flooring solution that is applied to concrete floors to create a smooth, tough surface that can withstand heavy traffic, spills, and other harsh conditions. It is made by combining two components resin and hardener which chemically bond to create a solid and resilient surface. Epoxy is commonly used in garages, basements, warehouses, hospitals, and retail spaces due to its strength, flexibility, and ease of maintenance. Epoxy flooring can be customized to include anti-slip additives, which make it safer for areas that experience moisture or heavy foot traffic. This is particularly useful in places like kitchens, bathrooms, and garages, where safety is a top concern.

Epoxy flooring is a great eco-friendly option because it is low in volatile organic compounds (VOCs), making it safe for both you and the environment. It is also long-lasting, which means less frequent replacements and less waste.
